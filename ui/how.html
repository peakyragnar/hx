How Heretix checks a claim

Heretix shows you what GPT‑5 already believes—its raw prior—before any web search or outside evidence. Here’s what happens each time you press “Ask the model.”

Step-by-step

  1. 1 · Freeze the lens We lock the model into the Raw Prior Lens. That means internal knowledge only: no browsing, no new documents, no citations.
  2. 2 · Ask many paraphrases Heretix rewrites your claim in several neutral wordings and asks GPT‑5 each one twice. This keeps wording quirks from tilting the answer.
  3. 3 · Aggregate the belief We average the responses in log-odds space, trim extreme outliers, and bootstrap a confidence interval so you see both the probability and how firm it is.
  4. 4 · Explain the lean Finally, we translate the result into plain English: the percentage, a verdict (“likely true/false/uncertain”), and 2–3 reasons the model leans that way.

What the percentage means

The number is GPT‑5’s probability that the claim is true. A 68% score means the model thinks it’s probably true; 11% means it thinks it’s probably false.

Advanced details

Open the “Advanced details” panel on any result to see:

  • Confidence width – how tight the probability interval is.
  • Template stability – whether different phrasings agreed.
  • Policy compliance – how often the responses followed Raw Prior rules.

Why no retrieval?

By skipping web searches we expose the model’s trained-in consensus. Later lenses will add evidence; this one lets you inspect the baseline belief first.

Tips

  • Be specific about the claim so the model isn’t guessing the scope.
  • Use the explanation bullets to decide what to investigate next.
  • Re-run anytime—cache makes repeats fast, so you can compare after prompt changes.
